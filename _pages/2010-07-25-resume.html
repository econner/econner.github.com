--- type: page layout: page title: Readings tags: [] meta: _wp_page_template: default _edit_last: "1" published: true status: publish --- Books / essays / articles that I enjoyed a lot. Hackers - Steven Levy Ender's Game - Orson Scott Card How to Win Friends and Influence People - Dale Carnegie Liking is for Cowards. Go for What Hurts. The Tragedy of the Commons - Hardin An Investigation of the Therac-25 Accidents Liar's Poker - Michael Lewis (recommended by Hari) A Million Miles in a Thousand Years - Donald Miller (recommended by Jess) Not my favorite book of all time, but a decent read.  I admire Miller's honesty and plain writing style. The Corrections - Jonathan Franzen (recommended by Robbie) Loved this book.  Franzen's witty style had me turning page after page.  One of my favorite metaphors: "It was a testament to the insulatory effectiveness of political boundaries that power didn’t simply arc across the gap between such divergent economic voltages."   His irony is fantastic.  Definitely recommend! A Whole New Mind - Daniel Pink (recommended by Gabe) Hackers and Painters - Paul Graham Chaos - James Gleick (recommended by Anu) When Turtles Fly - Nikki Stone (recommended by Anu) Logicomix Why People Think Computers Can't - Marvin Minsky (essay) Banker to the Poor - Muhammad Yunus The Black Swan - Nassim Nicholas Taleb On Intelligence - Jeff Hawkins The Age of Spiritual Machines - Ray Kurzweil Les Miserables - Victor Hugo This story could be my favorite book of all time, but I find it difficult to place why. The story is amazing, the theme incredible, and the writing beautiful. The best scene: Marius faints, near death while fighting in the bunker and Jean Valjean saves him. He saves the one he despises most but who his daughter loves. At once you feel the unique combination of passion and selflessness in Jean Valjean. White Noise - Don Delillo The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao - Junot Diaz The Electric Kool-Aid Acid Test - Tom Wolfe On the Road - Jack Kerouac Junky - William Burroughs
